Vehicles in Ratiba represent the rolling stock you bill to clients: 4x4 Land Cruisers, minivans, transfer cars, light aircraft (for the few operators who own them).

## Vehicle entries

Each vehicle has:

- **Name.** "Toyota Land Cruiser, 6-seat", "Toyota Hiace minibus, 9-seat", "Transfer sedan".
- **Capacity.** Max pax.
- **Pricing basis.**
  - **Per day.** Flat daily rate.
  - **Per kilometer.** Multiplied by the day's distance (you enter distance in the itinerary day editor).
  - **Per day + per km.** Both, summed.
- **Rate.** Day rate, km rate, or both.
- **Currency.**
- **Fuel included** (yes/no). Cosmetic flag, doesn't change pricing but appears in the proposal inclusions list.
- **Driver-guide included** (yes/no). Same.

## Picking a vehicle on the itinerary

When you set up a day, you pick a vehicle. Ratiba uses the same vehicle for the rest of the trip by default. Override per day if you swap vehicles for a transfer day.

## Capacity enforcement

If you place more pax in a vehicle than its capacity, Ratiba warns but doesn't block. Some operators routinely overload for short transfers — your judgment, your liability.

## Multi-vehicle trips

If you need two vehicles for one day (a large group), use the **Additional vehicle** option on the day editor. The second vehicle line is independent.

## Aircraft and transfers

Light aircraft (for charter flights) can be modeled as vehicles with a high day rate. Or treat them as transfers — see [Transfers](/docs/rate-cards/transfers).

We'll add a dedicated aircraft module if enough operators ask for it.
